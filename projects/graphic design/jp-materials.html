Japanese Teaching Materials

A collection of Japanese learning slides, charts, PDF worksheets, and visual explanations created for Alzenins, an online Japanese learning platform for Arabic-speaking students.

Project Context

These materials were created for students learning Japanese through Arabic. I designed the slides and learning files based on my teaching experience, Japanese knowledge, and understanding of what Arabic-speaking students usually struggle with.

My Role

I worked as teacher, content creator, and graphic designer. I planned the lessons, wrote Arabic explanations, prepared Japanese examples, and designed the visual layout.

Learning Focus

The materials cover hiragana, katakana, kanji, particles, verbs, adjectives, sentence structure, conversation, reading practice, and grammar patterns.

Visual Direction

The design needed to be clear, friendly, and easy to follow during live online lessons. I used color, spacing, tables, examples, and visual hierarchy to make complex grammar simpler.

AI Visual Support

Some illustrations and visual elements were created with AI tools. My work focused on lesson structure, content, layout, slide design, visual consistency, and final composition.

Writing Systems

Hiragana & Katakana Learning Slides

I designed materials that introduce the Japanese writing systems in a clear and visual way. The slides explain hiragana, katakana, kanji, main characters, derived sounds, long vowels, double consonants, and combined sounds.

These materials help beginners understand that Japanese writing is built from several systems, each with its own purpose and structure.

Tools: PowerPoint · Photoshop · Illustrator · AI Visual Tools

Grammar Slides

Grammar Explained in Arabic

I created grammar slides for topics such as nominal sentences, verbs, particles, adjectives, the ない form, たい・ほしい, から・まで, に・で, よ・ね・よね, and more.

The goal was to make grammar feel less abstract by using Arabic explanations, Japanese examples, translations, exercises, comparison tables, and short dialogues.

Tools: PowerPoint · Japanese Content Creation · Arabic Explanation

Kanji Materials

Kanji Introduction & Practice

The kanji materials explain what kanji is, where it comes from, why radicals are important, how stroke order works, and the difference between onyomi and kunyomi readings.

I designed the slides to reduce students’ fear of kanji by explaining the logic behind the characters before introducing practice pages and example words.

Tools: PowerPoint · Layout Design · Japanese Teaching

Conversation & Reading Files

Dialogue, Reading & Practice Materials

I also created conversation and reading materials where students can practice real examples, questions, answers, vocabulary, and short dialogue structures.

These files support live lessons and give students something practical to read, repeat, translate, and use in conversation.

Tools: PowerPoint · PDF Design · Arabic/Japanese Content

Design Process

01

Lesson Planning

I started by deciding what students needed to learn and what grammar or vocabulary should be explained in each lesson.

02

Content Writing

I wrote the Arabic explanations, Japanese examples, vocabulary lists, exercises, and practice sentences.

03

Visual Structure

I arranged the information using hierarchy, spacing, color, tables, and step-by-step examples to make the lessons easier to follow.

04

Classroom Use

The materials were created to be used directly during online teaching, so the design had to support explanation, interaction, and student practice.

Tools & Skills

This project combines teaching, Japanese language knowledge, Arabic explanation, educational design, slide design, layout, visual hierarchy, and AI-assisted visual work.
