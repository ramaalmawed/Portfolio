UX Research Thesis

“I Can’t Find the Filter – But the Site Looks Good” is my bachelor thesis in Digital Media at University West. The study explores how aesthetic and functional design principles affect the user experience on fashion e-commerce websites, using H&M and Zara as case studies.

Project Overview

This project focused on understanding how users experience online clothing stores. The study compared two different design directions: H&M’s more structured and functional interface, and Zara’s more visual, minimalistic and aesthetic interface.

Project Type

Bachelor thesis · UX research · E-commerce usability study

University

University West · Digital Media · Media Informatics · 2025

Case Studies

H&M and Zara fashion e-commerce websites.

Research Focus

Aesthetics, functionality, navigation, filtering, product presentation and usability.

Abstract

Research Summary

The aim of the thesis was to examine how the design of clothing retail websites affects user experience from both aesthetic and functional perspectives. The study compared Zara and H&M to identify which design elements helped or prevented users from interacting smoothly with the websites.

The results showed that H&M was generally experienced as more user-friendly because of its clear structure, simple navigation and easy-to-find functions. Zara was experienced as more visually appealing and premium, but several users had difficulties finding important functions such as filters and return information.

The conclusion was that successful web design should balance visual appeal with clear functionality. A website that focuses only on appearance but lacks usability risks creating frustration for users.

Research Question

Main Question

How do aesthetic and functional design principles affect the user experience on websites for clothing sales?

The thesis investigated how layout, typography, image use, information structure, search, filters and product presentation influence how users understand, navigate, feel and make decisions on fashion e-commerce websites.

Focus: UX Research · E-commerce · Aesthetic Design · Functional Design

How We Did It

The study used qualitative UX research methods: Think-Aloud, observation and semi-structured interviews. Participants completed realistic shopping tasks on both websites while their behaviour and reactions were observed.

01

Planning

We defined the research question, selected Zara and H&M as case studies, and planned realistic shopping tasks based on common e-commerce actions.

02

User Testing

Participants completed tasks such as finding clothing items, using filters, locating return information and adding products to the cart.

03

Think-Aloud

While navigating, participants verbalized what they saw, thought, liked, disliked or found confusing.

04

Observation

We observed behaviour, pauses, clicks, confusion, frustration, ease of use and emotional reactions during the tasks.

05

Interviews

After the tests, we conducted semi-structured interviews to understand participants’ experiences in more depth.

06

Analysis

We analyzed recurring patterns related to navigation, search, filtering, product images, return information, emotions and overall preference.

Key Findings

H&M

H&M was experienced as clearer, more practical and easier to navigate. Users found the structure more logical and many functions easier to understand.

Zara

Zara was experienced as more visually attractive and premium, but important functions such as search, filters and return information were sometimes harder to find.

Functionality

Clear navigation, visible filters, understandable categories and accessible information were important for completing shopping tasks smoothly.

Aesthetics

Visual design strongly affected first impressions, brand perception and emotional response, especially on Zara’s website.

Main Conclusion

The study showed that users want websites that are both visually attractive and easy to use. A beautiful interface can create interest and a premium feeling, but if users cannot find important functions, the experience can quickly become frustrating.

The best e-commerce experience is created when aesthetics and functionality work together: attractive visuals, clear navigation, accessible information and a logical shopping flow.

Important UX Insight

A website can look beautiful and still fail the user if key functions are hidden, unclear or difficult to understand.
